Associated Students of Linguistics

Info

ASOL is the official graduate student organization of UT Linguistics. We advocate for the interests of graduate students in departmental affairs, organize events relevant to graduate students' academic success, and provide a social support network.

Land Acknowledgment

We would like to acknowledge that we work and live on Indigenous land. Moreover, We would like to acknowledge and pay our respects to the Carrizo & Comecrudo, Coahuiltecan, Caddo, Tonkawa, Comanche, Lipan Apache, Alabama-Coushatta, Kickapoo, Tigua Pueblo, and all the American Indian and Indigenous Peoples and communities who have been or have become a part of these lands and territories in Texas, here on Turtle Island.

Anti-Racism Committee

Frances, Elijah, Sarah, Mike

Duties

Maintain a bibliography on the history of racist and anti-racist action in academia, maintain an informational document about the policies, connections, and endorsements of funding organizations, consult with conference/colloquia committees, instructors, and researchers about how to improve their practice, provide accountability to the steps outlined in the department’s anti-racism statement.

(This committee is being proposed by drafters of the Anti-Racism Statement in August 2020, with the hopes that it will be a collaborative effort between students and faculty.)
